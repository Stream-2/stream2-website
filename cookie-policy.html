1. What is a cookie?

Cookies are small files of information downloaded to a user's device when visiting a website. They are a tool used by web servers to store and retrieve information about their users. They have an expiry date, which can range from the length of the browsing session to a specified future date, after which they stop working. Cookies are essential to how the internet functions and cannot damage a user's device.

2. Types of cookies

By ownership:

  • First-party cookies: sent to your device from our own website.
  • Third-party cookies: sent from a domain not managed by us, but by another entity.

By purpose:

  • Functional cookies: allow navigation of the website and use of its various options or services.
  • Personalisation cookies: allow access to the service with predefined characteristics (language, browser, regional settings, etc.).
  • Analytics cookies: let us quantify the number of users and statistically analyse how they use our website, to improve our content and services.
  • Behavioural advertising cookies: store information about user behaviour to build a profile and show advertising based on it.

By duration:

  • Session cookies: collect and store data while the user accesses the website, and disappear when the session ends.
  • Persistent cookies: data remains stored on the device and may be processed for a defined period, from minutes to several years.

5. Managing cookies

You can block cookies through your browser's settings, or configure it to warn you when a server wants to store a cookie:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies. More at support.google.com.
  • Firefox: Options > Privacy > Cookies. More at support.mozilla.org.
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy. More at support.apple.com.
  • Edge/Internet Explorer: Settings > Privacy. More at windows.microsoft.com.

You may also exercise your right to block, delete or reject cookies, or change your consent at any time, via the "Cookie Settings" link in this website's footer.
